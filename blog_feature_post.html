Top 10 Mistakes Students Make Before Their Test Day

1. Sleeping Through Your Alarm

Ah, the classic blunder! Setting 3 alarms but still hitting snooze? Happens to the best of us. Tip: place your alarm across the room to force yourself out of bed.

2. Forgetting Your ID or Documents

You got all your notes and pens but left your ID at home. Trust me, it’s heartbreaking at the test center. Always pack your essentials the night before!

3. Last-Minute Cramming

Trying to memorize 100 vocabulary words the night before? Nope. Quick review is fine, but overloading your brain leads to panic.

4. Eating Junk Food

Pizza and soda for breakfast? Your brain deserves better! Go for a light, nutritious meal to stay sharp and focused.

5. Ignoring Test Instructions

Skipping the “Read this first” section can be disastrous. Take 2 minutes to understand the rules—it saves headaches later.

6. Over-Packing Your Bag

Bringing every notebook you own? Your bag will feel like a brick. Essentials only, folks!

7. Panicking Over Mock Scores

That one mock test doesn’t define you. Stay calm, review weak spots, and don’t stress over past scores.

8. Forgetting to Relax

Stressing out too much? Take a short walk, meditate, or listen to music. A relaxed mind works wonders.

9. Relying Only on Memory

Don’t just memorize formulas or words—practice applying them. Real test scenarios are different from your notes!

10. Ignoring Sleep the Night Before

Yes, that last-minute cramming might tempt you, but a well-rested brain beats an exhausted one any day. Sleep early and dream of victory!
